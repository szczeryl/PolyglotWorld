About Me

Having begun my language-learning journey when I was in middle school some years ago, I have discovered the pains of learning languages that have limtied resources online. One of my first endeavours was to pick up the Malay language, which I now can comfortably speak and even hold a conversation in. Yet, the biggest problem I faced was sourcing for resources online, as there are more resources for Indonesian than Malay online. While Indonesian is not too differnt from Malay, I understood that there were subtle differences, such as the meanings of the word budak in both languages.

Hence, I now proudly present to you Polyglot World, a site built by an aspiring polyglot for aspiring polyglots across the world. Across the years, I have dabbled in over a dozen languages, particpated in Linguistics Olympiads, and have even founded a language account on Instagram, garnering over 1000 followers.

My main goal is to make learning languages both accessible and easy in the process, for languages have proven to be moments of respite from my hectic everyday life. Personally, I am currently working on Vietnamese (yes, another Southeast Asian language) and wish to impart my knowledge in languages to other language learners out there.

About Polyglot World

As a university student, I am unfortunately too broke to pay others to work on this site with me. I work indepdendently, which hopefully explains the delays. I built this site locally using a combination of HTML, CSS and JS, with the brainchild of this site being from "ikindalikelanguages", another webpage that is very similar to this albeit with different content.

For now, the goal is to get the basics working before implemeting individualised accounts, audio inputs/outputs, as well as progress trackers.

I will link my GitHub repo here when I have published the site. Additionally, I am not a CS major so my code is probably not the most efficient or readable.
