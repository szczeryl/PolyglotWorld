Malay/Bahasa Melayu

Malay, or Bahasa Melayu, is an official language in Malaysia, Brunei, and Singapore. Similar to Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), it belongs to the Austronesian language family and is written using the Latin alphabet.

With over 290 million speakers, Malay is one of the most widely spoken languages in the world. It is known for its relatively straightforward grammar and lack of gendered nouns or complex verb conjugations, which makes it a popular and accessible language for learners.
