Lesson 3: Food

In this chapter, we will learn the names of common dishes in Malaysia. Malaysian cuisine is argurably one of the most diverse in the world. As a multiracial society, flavours from Malay, Southern Chinese and Tamilian cuisine have been incorporated, sometimes with a unique twist. Without further ado, I proudly present: Malaysian Food!

One of the most common staples you will find all around Asia is rice. "Nasi " is the word for rice in Malay. Do note that certain translators may generate the word "beras" instead. It isn't exactly wrong but beras refers to uncooked rice so one simply does not order uncooked rice at a restaurant.



Noodles are commonly found in Malaysia too. With many different variants, as long as you see "mee" on the menu, some kind of noodles will be found in that dish.



Bread is another food item that can be found all across the world. The word for bread is "roti" in Malay, with one of the most popular dishes being "Roti Canai", a kind of flatbread that originated from South India. If you were in Singapore, you will not find Roti Canai though. Instead, it will be called Roti Prata in its place.



Next up, we shall learn the proteins. As Malaysia has a large Muslim population, pork will only be found in non-halal food places, which can easily be found in larger cities. Here are some proteins you may come across.

Chicken: Ayam
Beef: Daging (translates to meat in Malay)
Fish: Ikan
Prawn: Udang
Squid: Sotong

Unlike English, adjectives come after the noun they are describing in Malay. Fried noodles will hence be directly translated to "noodle fried", or mee goreng in Malay.





Of course, not forgetting the other options. The blanket term for all vegetables in Malay is "sayur", while the word for egg is "telur". Soup is translated to "soto"



Of course, there are specialised terms for specific dishes, such as nasi lemak (the national dish), laksa, lontong, rendang, mee siam... Also, I happen to have a sweet tooth so learning the names of the different kuihs(cakes) are essential as well.
