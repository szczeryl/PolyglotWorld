Lesson 1: Greetings

With very few foreigners bothering to learn the language, locals are often pleasantly surprised even when you use the simplest Malay terms. A simple “good morning” or “thank you” can take you a long way!

Malay is written in the Latin alphabet, with no additional letters or accents. The letter “C” is pronounced like “ch” in “cheese.” Pronunciation is straightforward and can usually be derived directly from the spelling.

The goal of this course is to guide you through essential words for travel, such as greetings, numbers (for negotiating, of course), ordering food, and booking a hotel. You will also learn some useful everyday vocabulary, including common food and drink items. While there are slight variations between countries and regions, this course focuses on everyday conversational Malay that will help you blend in like a local.

Lesson 2

In Malay, "hai" means "hello". Sometimes, people may say "Assalamualaikum" instead, which is derived from Arabic. Regardless, a simple "hai" or even "hello" works as most people in Malaysia are able to speak some English.



Next up, we will learn how to say "good morning". "Good morning" is "selamat pagi". Pagi means morning while selamat means safe. It is however used for the greetings.



"afternoon" or "evening" is "petang". This refers to anytime between 12pm to sunset. "Night" is "malam", which can be used anytime from sunset till nightfall.




"Thank you" in Malay is "terima kasih". "terima" means to receive or accept and "kasih" means love. Essentially, you thank someone because you received their love.



After someone thanks you, you will express your gratitude by saying "sama-sama". "Sama" means same in Malay. In Malay, you pluralise words by repeating them again. Hence, "sama-sama" can be translated to "same same".



At last, saying goodbye! In Malay, there are two ways to say goodbye. If you were hosting a party and your guests are leaving, you say "selamat jalan", which translates to "have a safe journey". However, if you were leaving a place instead, you will tell the host "selamat tinggal", which translates to "have a good stay".




And that's all for lesson 1. See you at lesson 2!
